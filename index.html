More grand tourer than sports car, the Dodge Challenger is an unapologetically brash two-door coupe in the grand tradition of American muscle. Revived in 2008 as a direct successor to the 1970-1974 first generation model, the Challenger soldiers on today in its 12th year of production. But how to improve on the basic formula? More horsepower, of course. There are no fewer than four V-8 options available for the 2020 Dodge Challenger, starting with the 375-hp 5.7-liter Hemi and topping out with the 797-hp SRT Hellcat Redeye, and all feature a standard six-speed manual transmission. Dodge balances out this bold swagger with a dash of sensibility: V-6-powered Challengers offer available all-wheel drive. Who says you can't teach an old dog new tricks?
